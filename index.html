Designing for Emerging Technologies


AI-Assisted Real-Estate Brokerage at Bramble

Reimagining Real Estate with Bramble

How do you design trust into a product promising to save users tens of thousands of dollars? That was the core question guiding our team—Jowet, Annie, Selena, and I—as we tackled the redesign of Bramble’s desktop website. As a new AI-powered real estate brokerage with bold claims and cutting-edge technology, Bramble needed an interface that could communicate credibility and innovation while supporting users through one of life’s biggest financial decisions.


About Bramble

Bramble is a modern, AI-powered real estate brokerage that replaces the traditional 2–3% agent commission with a transparent flat fee. It offers end-to-end buying and selling services designed to maximize value for both sides of the transaction. On average, buyers receive a rebate of around $20,000—all while maintaining support from licensed human agents.


Key features include:

  • AI semantic search to match listings to user intent
  • AI-generated video tours based on listing photographs
  • Flat-fee structure that promotes affordability and transparency
  • Licensed human agents for ongoing guidance and support

Design Challenge

We wanted the interface to reflect Bramble’s innovative approach while establishing trust—especially for users encountering these ideas for the first time. Buying or selling a home is a significant decision, and we needed users to feel confident using a platform that challenges traditional norms.

Our guiding question: How can we emphasize what makes Bramble new and exciting, while keeping the experience grounded and reliable?


We aligned on three key design goals to strike that balance:

  • Clearly highlight the flat-fee rebate model
  • Make search intuitive and guided
  • Empower buyers and sellers from start to finish

Incorporating Feedback Into Prototype

While developing our Figma prototype, we sought feedback from TAs to refine our vision.


Feedback From TAs

  • We initially presented a non-scrollable homepage which felt overwhelming. We redesigned it into a scrollable layout, placing the brand message, search bar, and key differentiators up front.
  • To clarify Bramble's use of AI, we added a dedicated AI workflow section to the homepage.
  • We chose not to include visuals in the how-to guide to avoid overcrowding, relying on strong textual explanation instead.
  • House cards were made more clearly clickable with an expand button.
  • We received positive feedback on the plan to include About and Sell pages in the hi-fi prototype.

CEO Feedback & Future Directions

CEO Insights

  • Suggested using a more versatile secondary color; Bramble berry works better as an accent than a background.
  • Recommended clarifying search bar functionality and highlighting search modes (address, link, natural language).
  • Suggested simplifying filters and expanded home view UI for clarity.
  • Encouraged use of visual enhancements and exploring video integration on property displays.

Planned Improvements

  • Build out the Sellers page to include seller-side workflows (limited feedback from CEO on this).
  • Add more listings to encourage AI feature engagement.
  • Make search and filter functionality fully interactive.
  • Implement the CEO’s visual and usability suggestions if given more time.

User Testing

Once we iterated on our prototype, we conducted user tests to analyze usabiity of an essential overarching task.


Core Task

Find a home based on your personal preferences and view full listing details. This task reflects Bramble's core value: enabling users to find suitable homes efficiently with AI-powered tools.


Findings

  • Users repeatedly tried to use the inactive search bar and wanted more advanced filters (e.g., schools nearby).
  • AI functionality was intriguing but not clearly surfaced—many users missed or skipped the explanatory section.
  • Despite some non-functional elements, all users successfully navigated to a listing and completed the task. Aesthetic and layout were consistently praised.

Reflection

Throughout our iterative design process, our team—Jowet, Annie, Selena, and I—learned to balance user needs with business goals and embrace design feedback loops. Key takeaways include:

  1. We merged diverse ideas into a wireframe that aligned with Bramble’s mission.
  2. We made layout and clarity changes after TA feedback (e.g., scrollable homepage, more intuitive cards).
  3. We clarified AI integration after user confusion, adding a dedicated section informed by a CEO interview.
  4. User testing validated usability and helped refine interactive priorities.
  5. Final feedback from the CEO confirmed that we accurately conveyed Bramble’s value and mission.

Had we more time, we would continue developing interactivity, iterate on the color palette, and focus on filter functionality. Above all, this project showed us the value of user-centered iteration and responsiveness to critique.

Rahel Selemon, CSCI1300 Spring 2025
